# Filament Alert Prototype

![Filament Alert Prototype](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/SDO_SDO_20120831_171.304.blend.jpg)

The prototype uses a single rule: the alert should explain the next action before it explains the phenomenon. Most science displays do the reverse and then wonder why operators ignore them.

In the review build, the alert card has one threshold, one confidence line, and one instruction: keep the public display in delayed mode until the image stream stabilizes. The deeper science note remains one click away.

We tested three verbs: watch, hold, and switch. Watch felt passive. Hold implied danger. Switch gave the operator a real task without overselling the event.

A warning that cannot be acted on is just weather in a louder voice.
